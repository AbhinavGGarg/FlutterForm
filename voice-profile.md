# Voice profile — Abhinav

Built from how Abhinav writes in chat (casual, low-stakes). Capture the style, not the topics.

## Fingerprints
- **Capitalization:** mostly lowercase, including sentence starts ("so is it good?", "yup do that", "nope we need"). Uses ALL CAPS for emphasis ("the BEST", "I MEAN THE BEST", "WHICH modes"). Proper nouns capitalized.
- **Punctuation:** minimal. Commas to stack clauses into run-ons ("is this actually good, worth it, like would they take this?"). Lots of question marks. No em dashes, no semicolons. Often drops apostrophes ("im", "your"=you're, "ill", "dont", "everyting").
- **Diction / slang:** very casual — "u", "ur", "wtv wtv", "bro", "idk", "prolly", "kinda", "sort of thing", "yup", "nope", "gonna".
- **Fillers / hedges:** "like" (filler), "idk", "sort of thing", "I mean".
- **Sentence length:** short, punchy, sometimes fragments. Stacks ideas with commas. Asks a lot of direct questions.
- **Stance / energy:** blunt, direct, impatient, goal-focused. Enthusiastic (caps emphasis) but skeptical — repeatedly pushes on "is it ACTUALLY good / worth it." Values honesty over hype. Not corporate at all.
- **Imperfections:** leaves typos and missing apostrophes in, doesn't sweat them.

## Writing for him
- Lead with the honest thing; don't oversell (he'll smell it).
- Keep it short and direct. Cut throat-clearing.
- Casual diction is fine; dial formality up for public posts but keep the blunt, plain rhythm and the honesty.
- Never corporate-LinkedIn ("thrilled to announce", "grateful for the opportunity", "journey") and never AI-slop.
- Emphasis via the occasional caps word, not bold or emoji-spam.
